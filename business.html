Who needs workers’ compensation insurance?
  • Any business with one or more employees
  • Companies in high-risk industries
  • Employers with full-time, part-time, or seasonal workers
  • Businesses with independent contractors (depending on state laws)
Why is
workers' comp required?

Workers’ compensation is
required by law in every US
state (other than Texas).

Not having workers’ compensation means you are exposed to:
  • Legal penalty (you could be personally held liable for injuries/fatalities)
  • Fines (states impose fines that can reach thousands of dollars per day for every uninsured employee)
  • Lawsuits (an injured employee could file a lawsuit for medical bills and lost wages)
  • Bankruptcy (one severe injury/fatality could result in a multimillion-dollar settlement)

70% of lost time is medically unnecessary. The longer an injured worker is out, the less likely they are to return to full duty. 

That’s why we work hand-in-hand with medical professionals to help employees return to productive work sooner, and to their normal work duties, as quickly as possible

How to reduce workers' compensation costs

We tackle the at-home-sick-because-I-got-
workers-comp syndrome by working with:

  • A workability mindset:

    • What can the injured worker do as opposed to what can’t they do.

  • A team of medical experts:

    • How can the injured worker stay active and heal faster?

  • A forward-thinking drive:

    • We proactively move a claim forward and never allow it to linger unattended

  • A solid back-to-work program:

    • We reduce lost time by getting the employee back to modified duty as they work their way back to full responsibilities
